# Problem Framing Canvas: Entrepreneur Email Triage

**Date:** 2026-04-13  
**Source:** `docs/product-vision.md` + in-chat canvas responses

---

## Phase 1: Look Inward

### What is the problem? (Symptoms)
Entrepreneurs (especially at small companies) can feel overwhelmed by high volumes of email coming from many directions (investors, customers, partners, etc.), making it difficult to organize and prioritize their inbox effectively.

### Why haven't we solved it?
There are existing tools (for example, Gemini integrated into Gmail), but they are general-purpose. There isn’t a tool clearly tailored to entrepreneurs’ specific workflows and needs.

### How are we part of the problem? (Assumptions & biases)
Our perspective is shaped by our network (mostly students) and seeing peers founding companies (especially AI companies). Because of that, we may be overweighting how a similar age group experiences the problem and may not yet see the full picture across other founder demographics.

**Which of these might be redesigned, reframed, or removed?**
We should deliberately widen discovery beyond our immediate peer group to avoid building only for “student/young technical founders,” and validate whether entrepreneur-specific context and workflows vary significantly by stage, industry, and background.

---

## Phase 2: Look Outward

### Who experiences the problem? (When, where, consequences)
**Who:** Founding members of up-and-coming companies, especially technical founders who are less familiar with business processes (for example, setting up investor meetings).

**When/Where:** In day-to-day inbox management while juggling product development plus external commitments and coordination.

**Consequences:** Time and attention are pulled away from actually building the product due to manual triage and follow-ups for outside commitments.

### Who else has this problem? Who doesn't have it?
**Who else has it:** Most industries and roles that rely on email communication (it’s broadly applicable beyond tech).

**How they deal with it:** Manual email review or generic AI tools that can miss important thread context.

**Who doesn't have it:** Few people; most knowledge workers who use email experience this at some level.

### Who's been left out? Who benefits?
**Who's been left out:** People in regions with limited internet access; and roles that are less email-centric (for example, some blue-collar/manual jobs such as farming).

**Who benefits when problem exists:** Assistants and roles whose job includes managing executives’/leaders’ inboxes and scheduling.

**Who benefits when problem doesn't exist:** Founders and small teams who regain time and focus; customers/investors who get more timely, higher-quality responses. (Some assistant roles may be reduced or changed.)

---

## Phase 3: Reframe

### Stated another way, the problem is:
Early-stage founders—especially technical founders—struggle to consistently identify, prioritize, and act on critical emails because today’s tools are general-purpose and don’t adapt to entrepreneur-specific workflows and context. This pulls time away from product-building and increases the risk of dropped follow-ups and missed opportunities. Our current understanding may be biased toward younger/student-founder networks, so the framing should be validated across a broader range of entrepreneurs.

### How Might We...
**How might we** adapt email triage to each founder’s business context and workflows to surface action-ready insights from email threads **as we aim to** protect founders’ product-building time and improve response quality and speed?
